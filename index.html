About Me

I am currently a graduate researcher at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center and Boston Children's Hospital, working under Dr. Sizun Jiang and Dr. Brian Kalish. My work involves building computational pipelines for spatial transcriptomics (Xenium, MERFISH) to study neural stem cell niches and tumor-immune microenvironments.

Previously, at the University of Toronto, I developed transformer-based models (3UTRBERT) for RNA regulation and investigated perinatal brain injury. I am passionate about developing open-source tools that make complex spatial data accessible and interpretable.

Research Projects

View GitHub

SpatialAssignR

An R package automating anatomical region annotation in MERFISH datasets using clustering and KNN voting. Reduced boundary errors by ~90% and annotation time to under 1 minute.

R Spatial Omics Clustering

Differential Proximity Analysis

Developed a novel computational method in R for inferring condition-associated differences in cell type spatial proximity. This framework utilizes Linear Mixed-Effects Models (LME) to quantify shifts in cellular microenvironments while accounting for biological variability. It enabled the identification of niche-specific changes in cellular architecture, including sex-specific spatial reorganization of microglia and neural progenitors, as detailed in Nature Neuroscience.

R Spatial Statistics LME

3UTRBERT

Co-developed a Transformer-based deep learning model for 3'UTR-mediated gene regulation. Improved AUROC to 0.836 across 22 CLIP-seq datasets.

Python PyTorch Transformers

Cross-Modal Imputation

Integrated scRNA-seq with low-plex spatial data to expand feature space from ~500 to >20,000 genes, recovering 70% of masked genes and enabling spatial mapping of autism-risk loci.

Integration scRNA-seq
